The Passion of Punctuation

5.0 stars
(1)
View Resource
  • Report this resource

Description

Overview:
Using published writers' texts and students' own writing, this unit explores emotions that are associated with the artful and deliberate use of commas, semicolons, colons, and exclamation points (end-stop marks of punctuation).
Subject:
Language, Grammar and Vocabulary, Reading Foundation Skills, Reading Informational Text, Reading Literature, Speaking and Listening
Level:
High School
Grades:
Grade 9, Grade 10
Material Type:
Activity/Lab, Unit of Study
Provider:
ReadWriteThink
Provider Set:
ReadWriteThink
Date Added:
08/29/2013

Language:

Comments

Bob Young on Jan 12, 12:04pm

I implemented this unit in December 2015 with my freshmen English class. I utilized Session One over a week and a half. My students are almost entirely Tier II students. They enjoyed and found value in the emoticon lesson. They were able to complete the Marks of Punctuation: What's Your Attitude? table. Overall, the reading selections were too high level for them. The video selections were engaging and effective except for the Cormac McCarthy interview with Oprah Winfrey.
In order to make it work again, I would have to use it as part of a larger grammar/punctuation series. I would need to find reading selections that are more appropriate to my students.

Sarah Reser on Jan 12, 11:58am

I attempted to use this resource in my 6th grade classroom. I would use this resource again, however my students were unable to comprehend the more difficult texts. When I do this again next year, I will modify it to fit my needs. I will keep the emoji introduction activity, the video clips and the JK Rowling edit activity.

Ambra Bryant on Jan 12, 11:58am

I have found that this resource had some very interesting and interactive components. One thing I will note is that there was a strong need for additional instruction and building of background knowledge. If your students already have a solid foundation of grammar and punctuation I highly recommend this resource as it brings punctuation to life. However, if you have not already explicitly taught these concepts you are going to want to do that intentionally prior to implementing this resource. My kids and I especially loved the emoji exercise and how that opened up conversation for emotion and style in writing through punctuation.

Brendan Johnson on Jan 11, 10:41pm

I implemented parts of Lesson's 1-4 (October 2015). I started with Emoticons and 'What's Your Attitude?' chart. Next, I utilized our classroom notebooks with several documents in the resource coupled with partner and group activities. I ended (January 2016) with 'You've Got Style' and the Postcard Creation + Written Response. Students engaged with the lessons that required revision/editing while choosing the most appropriate choice of punctuation from (colon, exclamation point, comma and coordinating conjunction. or semicolon). Rationalizing why a particular choice was made proved to be beneficial for students learning correct or preferred mechanics.
